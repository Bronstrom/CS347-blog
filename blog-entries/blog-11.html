Week 11 (12/10/21): Teaching Web Dev (& Closing Remarks)

For my final blog of the semester, I've been asked "How would you teach web development class if you were the professor?" (Or something along those lines.)

How would you design the course, my good sir?

I enjoyed the content very much in the class this semester. I honestly thought all the content was fairly relevant. If there were any cuts I would make to the class, I would say shorten the intro to HTML & CSS. I do feel like these are quite essential to the course, but I feel like students can pick up on it relatively quick. Also, probably if not all students, the vast majority of students have some previous experience with it. I would say focus I little more on creating web services and web frameworks. Maybe even include a short introduction to another framework besides React, like Vue or Angular.

In the professor's shoes, what would you have your students do?

I'm personally a huge fan of lectures, but I feel like a hand on experience is valuable to keeping the students engaded and learning. If the class was set on a two day a week schedule, I would prefer to have a lecture the first day of the week and then apply that newly gained knowledge to a lab. Another idea I had would be to split each class in half. To keep the information fresh for students, the earlier half could be a lecture and then the latter half have the students do an exercise. Although, labs would be a lot shorter in this case, and only a single task would sufice - versus two or three.

Based on experience, what helped you learn the material in Web Dev (CS347) and any advice to provide to future students?

I would say what I found most useful about the material were the demos provided in Prof. Chris Johnson's online textbook, All Over the Web. It was honestly really nice to see an example of how you would put together a web service and an approach of how you can set it up. Most of the time, I would follow along the demo's for practice and make a similar service with a few additions/modifications. I would recommend others to do the same

Most students might not appreciate this next point as much - because it's a weekly activity they might dread - but in my option, putting together this very blog and learning something new each week enforces a very important concept to web dev and CS as a whole. With advancing software & technology, new things are always being discovered and built. It's important to be savy in arising tech and explore. Don't be so reliant on professors/teachers spoon feeding information to you.
