Week 8 (11/12/21): Tooltips

Let's explore a simple but powerful feature to add to a website: tooltips.

Can you Briefly Describe What a "Tooltip" Is?

A tooltip is a short message or box appearing when a user interacts with an element on a page. Tooltips can be found in the form of announcements on pages to guide you where to click/navigigate or even simply the text information that appears when you hover over an icon. The tooltip is a microinteraction or furthermore a dynamic process. This type of process occurs with a trigger, causing an action to happen and provides the user feedback. Tooltips can be used to provide additional information, and help prevent an over crowded screen. They can also be used to give a little extra UX flare.

Are There any Precausion to Take When Using Tooltips?

Very much so. There is a fine line between a good tooltip and poor implemented tooltip. Now, we want to use tooltips carefully and avoid them being a nusance. In a way it may seem as if our tooltip is like a nuanced cousin of the weary and obsolete pop-up window. To achieve this point, tooltips should be used in small scale contexts where it is subtle. According to the designer, Marc Schenker, the better tooltips "are so discreet that, sometimes, youâ€™d swear they were never there".

Tootips can be observed across many popular sites today, including Facebook and Twitter. They are also popularly used in onbarding courses to communicate clear and helpful information. Tooltips on these sites or software don't go overtop it's simple purpose of providing a little more information and potentially boosting the UX design a little. Although they may be observed on many popular sites, they are not needed everwhere. Tooltips should fit a specific purpose. If extra details are not needed, it would probably be best to ditch using a tooltip. The feature of a tooltip also shouldn't be overused to the point where it's tedious for the user to obtrain information by continuously triggering a tooltip to appear.

I'm Guessing you Have Some Design Tips, Right?

You got it. See the following rules below in achieving a great tooltip:

  • Minimalistic Design - use basic colors as well as clear information for the user
  • Obvious Cue - make the trigger for the tooltip to appear obvious to the user. Also, avoid making it distracting.
  • Sustainable - the tooltip and trigger should be consistently noticable and unobtrusive
  • Relevant - supplement information in the right context and don't repeat information what was provided already
