Week 9 (11/17/21): Dark Mode

Today, we'll discuss the popular and trendy dark mode theme for webpages.

Can you Give me a Remark on the Mode of the Dark?

It might be obvious but in case you don't know, dark mode or dark theme is a mode for viewing content in a mostly dark atmosphere on the UI. Dark mode reduces the amount of light emitted by your viewing screen, while at the same time including enough contrast for content to be viewed well.

Generally, dark mode may include a dark background color, like a dark grey, with bright text layered on top. On the opposite end of the spectrum, light mode includes a white background color, with black or dark colored text.

What are the perks and losses of Dark Mode?

Pros:

  • Saves energy/power - especially for devices with OLED and AMOLED displays
  • Tends to reduce eye strain in low light conditions
  • A darker screen reduces glare, furthermore, minimizing blue and flickering light

Cons:

  • In some situations, it can inhibit eye strain instead of reducing it - such as in blight conditions where text is difficult to read
  • Light Mode generally has better readability - especially for longer strings of text

How can I Enable a Webpage to be in Dark Mode?

Ocassionally you can see a toggle for light and dark mode with a button on a well built webpage. There may be an accessibility control button hidden in the corner, giving the option.

Although, more often than not, dark mode can be choosen at the operating system level in system settings/preferences. On many popular browsers, like Firefox and Chrome, they include a browser option to change to a dark theme. Furthermore, on some browsers you can toggle dark and light theme in the developer tools or inspector of a webpage when the webpage has an enabled dark and light theme.

Any Ideal Design Choices to Make When Including this Mode?

Implementing a dark mode may seem fairly simple, although it's a little more challenging to design one. Here are a couple points to pay attention to when designing a dark mode:

Add a Dark Filter for Images

Decreasing the brightness and contrast of images on a page is much better than jarring contrast of bright images.

Find a balanced Style for the Typography

Similar to images, the contrast needs to be balanced. Find a midpoint between too heavy and bold of a font and too thin and condensed of a font that will strain someone's eyes.

Make Dark Mode Colors Softer

Pure white text ontop of a pure black can be jarring. An off-white can do the trick here and a dark gray, like #121212 as suggested by the Materal Design Dark Theme Guidelines.
