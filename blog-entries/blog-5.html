Week 5 (10/8/21): Reaching an Audience

At the forefront of creating any webpage, the audience should be one of major factors to consider most of all. They are the main reason you're creating a site.

How does Audience Impact a Website?

By knowing your audience, the designer of a webpage can identify and make key decisions to make the site successful. It's important to cater options and features that the target audience desires in a site. Starting with a narrow scope to a key audience is important to getting off to the right foot of advertising/selling a product or service. This reach can always be expanded opon later.

What Cards Should I Play When Trying to Reach an Audience?

I'll list some key topics that will impact the type of audience below.

Writing Content & Tone

The voice of the writer and vocabulary helps tailor to an audience. A more conversational tone may make more sense to an adult audience, while content for younger ages include a slimmed down vocabulary and are more direct to the point. It's also important to avoid using jaragon unknown to the target audience and break down unfimiliar topics.

Navigation

Touching back on Week 3's topic, Navigation, we see some overlap in the importance of it's impact on the audience. The content the audience is seeking should be prioritized, instead of having it hidden down a narrow path of links. Ideally, the menu options guide the audience directly where to go. Furthermore, the navigation should by intuitive and simple for any audience to figure out.

Layout

Keep the layout of elements on a webpage straight-forward and simple. Avoid designing with many columns and elements showing up on a screen at a time. A cluttered screen can overwelm the audience and be distracting. This topic also loops back to Negative Space, as discussed in Week 2.

Font

Typically, san-serif fonts are used for headings and sefit fonts are used for the paragraph. Although, the style choices will heavily rely on the brand and audience. A stylish font, often goes the the youthful route, while simpler fonts are observed as more professional and prefered by older audiences.

Theme & Color

When thinking of theme and color of the website, a web designer should make distict choices seperating the site from other brands. Ideally, the audience should be able to associate the theme with the brand right away. The theme is often what makes the first impression of a webpage.

Accessibility

When design a webpage, go the step of making it accessible to all. Today, accessiblity features are required by law. By providing alt tags to images, screen readers can understand the content better. Adding a dark theme to a website also increases accessiblity.

How Can I Determine my Audience?

Also, you should question if there is already a well-sized audience, but you want to reach out to more. Create a sample customer profile - determine their age, gender, background, income level, and location. Research the market and competitors. Site statistics can give the designer a good idea of what type of traffic they are recieving, and what information the audience is looking for. This will provide an idea of patterns.

Also, you may be thinking, I don't want an audience! If there was no audience what is the point of creating a site? Maybe just practice? Create an ideal customer profile or determine what similar businesses do. Also, in a sense, you make up the audience here. This website will benefit your knowledge.
